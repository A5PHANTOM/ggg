CUTTING-DIET

aaa

A cutting diet is focused on reducing body fat while preserving muscle, commonly followed by bodybuilders and fitness enthusiasts during a "cutting phase." The key is to create a calorie deficit by eating fewer calories than the body requires, while maintaining a high protein intake to protect muscle mass.

Key Principles of a Cutting Diet:

  • High Protein: Aim for lean protein sources to preserve muscle mass.
  • Low to Moderate Carbs: Focus on slow-digesting carbs like whole grains, and reduce overall carb intake, especially refined carbs and sugars.
  • Healthy Fats: Incorporate fats from good sources like nuts, seeds, and ghee in moderation.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Meal Timing: Eat smaller, frequent meals throughout the day to keep metabolism active.

SUGGESTED RECIPES

Breakfast

  • Egg whites omelette (with spinach, tomatoes, onions) cooked in minimal olive oil.
  • 1 slice of whole wheat bread or multigrain roti.
  • Black coffee or green tea (no sugar).
  • Scrambled eggs (2 whole eggs + 3 egg whites).
  • 1 bowl of oats with almond milk, a few nuts, and seeds.
  • Grilled chicken breast or fish fillet (seasoned with Indian spices like turmeric, cumin, and coriander).
  • 1 roti or a small bowl of brown rice.

Lunch

  • Grilled/boiled chicken breast or grilled fish.
  • A large portion of salad (cucumber, broccoli, tomatoes, lettuce, onions, and olive oil dressing).
  • 1-2 rotis (multigrain).
  • Tandoori chicken (skinless, marinated in curd and spices, baked or grilled).
  • Sautéed vegetables (spinach, bell peppers, broccoli) with garlic.
  • Brown rice (small portion).
  • Chicken curry (made with minimal oil, without cream).
  • Brown rice or quinoa (small portion).
